I'm a software engineer in Cluj. I've been reviewing school curricula the same way I review tech stacks — systematically, with criteria.

I visited fourteen schools. Not because I'm indecisive. Because I know what I'm looking for.

At most of them, the approach is: sit still, memorize this, repeat it back. My kid would be bored in a week. I know what that does to a child who actually wants to think.

At Colina, my son came home on the third day and explained to me — unprompted, at dinner — how bridges are built. He built a model. He cared about it.

That's not a school thing. That's a parenting win.

The commute to Băbușiu is long. We do it anyway.
