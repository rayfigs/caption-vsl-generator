We didn't plan to be here.

We packed everything, we moved countries, we told ourselves it would all figure itself out. And then day one, someone asked us where our daughter would go to school.

And I didn't have an answer.

The first few months were hard. She missed her friends. We missed ours. Everything was unfamiliar.

But then something shifted. She started coming home from school with things to say. Real things. Not "fine," not "nothing." Actual stories.

I don't know how long we'll be in Romania. But wherever we land next, she'll carry what she learned here.

That matters more than I expected it to.
